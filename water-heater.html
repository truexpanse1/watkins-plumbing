Complete Water Heater Services for Every Home

Signs You Need Water Heater Service

  • No hot water — The most obvious sign. Your water heater has stopped heating entirely
  • Discolored water — Rusty, brown, or murky hot water indicates corrosion inside the tank
  • Strange noises — Popping, banging, or rumbling sounds mean sediment buildup on the heating elements
  • Leaking — Water pooling around the base of the unit signals a cracked tank or failed fitting
  • Age 10+ years — Most tank water heaters last 8-12 years. If yours is over 10, it's time to plan ahead
  • Higher energy bills — A failing water heater works harder and uses more energy to heat the same amount of water

Our Water Heater Service Process

  1. Diagnose: We inspect your water heater thoroughly — checking the thermostat, heating elements, anode rod, pressure relief valve, and connections to pinpoint the exact issue.
  2. Recommend Repair or Replace: Based on the age, condition, and repair cost, we give you an honest recommendation. We explain the pros and cons of each option so you can make an informed decision.
  3. Upfront Quote: You get a written quote before we touch a wrench. No hidden charges, no surprise add-ons. The price we quote is the price you pay.
  4. Install or Repair: Our licensed technicians complete the work efficiently, following all local building codes and manufacturer specifications. New installations include proper venting, gas/electrical connections, and seismic strapping.
  5. Test: We verify the water heater is operating correctly — checking temperature output, pressure, gas connections (for leaks), and proper drainage. We don't leave until you have reliable hot water.

Water Heater FAQs

Common questions about water heater repair and installation.

As a general rule, if your water heater is under 8 years old and the repair cost is less than 50% of a new unit, repair usually makes sense. If it's over 10 years old, has recurring problems, or the tank is leaking, replacement is typically the better investment. We'll give you an honest recommendation based on your specific situation.

Tankless water heaters cost more upfront but can save you 20-30% on water heating costs and last 20+ years (vs. 8-12 for tank units). They provide unlimited hot water on demand and take up much less space. If you plan to stay in your home long-term, a tankless unit often pays for itself within 5-7 years.

A standard tank-to-tank replacement typically takes 2-4 hours. If you're upgrading from a tank to a tankless unit, the installation may take 4-8 hours due to additional gas line, venting, and electrical work required. We complete most installations in a single visit.

A new tank water heater (installed) typically runs $1,200-$2,500 depending on the size and fuel type. Tankless water heaters range from $2,500-$4,500 installed. We provide detailed, upfront quotes so you know exactly what to expect — no surprises on the bill.
