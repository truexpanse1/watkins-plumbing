Water Line Repair & Replacement in Chico & Butte County

Your water line is the lifeline of your home's plumbing system — it carries fresh water from the city main to every faucet, shower, and appliance in your house. When it develops a leak, corrodes, or fails, the damage can be extensive and expensive if not addressed quickly. Whether you need main water line repair or emergency water pipe burst repair, our licensed team responds fast.

At Watkins Plumbing, we specialize in detecting and fixing water line problems using advanced leak detection equipment. Whether you have a small pinhole water line leak repair or need a complete water service line replacement, our licensed technicians will diagnose the issue accurately and get your water flowing again. We also handle underground water line repair for pipes buried beneath driveways, landscaping, and foundations.

We work with all pipe materials including copper pipe repair, PEX, and polyethylene, and handle everything from spot repairs to full main line replacements. Our team also manages main line connections to the city water supply, ensuring all work meets local codes and passes inspection. If you're dealing with a water main break repair emergency, we offer same-day service.

Water line issues don't get better on their own — they get worse. If you've noticed unexplained puddles, discolored water, low water pressure repair needs, or a spike in your water bill, call Watkins Plumbing today for a thorough inspection and honest recommendation.

Signs You Need Main Water Line Repair

  • Unexplained puddles or soggy areas in your yard
  • Discolored or rusty water coming from your taps
  • Noticeably low water pressure throughout the house
  • Foundation cracks or shifting that may indicate underground leaks
  • Unexpectedly higher water bills without increased usage
  • Wet spots on walls, ceilings, or floors with no visible source

Our Water Line Repair Process

  1. Locate the Leak: We use professional leak detection equipment including acoustic sensors and camera technology to pinpoint the exact location of the problem without unnecessary digging.
  2. Assess the Damage: Once located, we evaluate the extent of the damage — is it a single leak, corrosion along a section, or a failing line that needs full replacement?
  3. Discuss Repair vs. Replace: We give you an honest, upfront assessment of whether a targeted repair will solve the problem or if a full replacement is the smarter long-term investment.
  4. Complete the Work: Our licensed technicians perform the repair or replacement using quality materials (copper, PEX, or polyethylene) suited to your home and budget.
  5. Pressure Test: We run a comprehensive pressure test on the repaired or new line to verify there are zero leaks and water flow is fully restored.
  6. Restore Landscaping: If excavation was required, we backfill and restore the affected area so your yard looks as close to original as possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a water line leak include unexplained wet spots in your yard, a sudden increase in your water bill, low water pressure, discolored water, or the sound of running water when all fixtures are off. If you notice any of these signs, call us for a professional leak detection — we can locate the exact problem without tearing up your property.
A spot repair on a water line typically ranges from $300 to $1,500 depending on depth, location, and pipe material. A full water line replacement usually runs $2,000 to $6,000+ depending on the length of the line, pipe material chosen, and excavation required. We always provide an upfront quote so you know the exact cost before we start.
A simple spot repair can often be completed in 2 to 4 hours. A full water line replacement typically takes 1 to 2 days depending on the length of the line, depth of the pipe, and whether concrete or landscaping needs to be removed and restored. We'll give you a clear timeline upfront.
It depends on your policy and the cause of the damage. Most standard homeowner's insurance policies cover sudden and accidental water line breaks, but not damage caused by age, wear, or poor maintenance. We recommend checking with your insurance provider. We can provide detailed documentation and photos of the damage to support your claim if needed.
