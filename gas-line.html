Signs of a Gas Leak Emergency

  • Rotten egg smell — Natural gas is odorless, but utility companies add mercaptan (a sulfur-like odor) so you can detect leaks. If you smell rotten eggs, you may have a gas leak
  • Hissing near gas line — A hissing or whistling sound near a gas pipe, appliance connection, or meter indicates gas escaping under pressure
  • Dead plants near the line — If vegetation is dying in a line pattern across your yard, an underground gas line may be leaking and killing the roots
  • Higher gas bills — An unexplained increase in your gas bill could mean gas is leaking from a pipe before it reaches your appliances
  • Pilot light keeps going out — A pilot light that repeatedly goes out may indicate inconsistent gas pressure from a leak or damaged line

Our Gas Leak Detection & Repair Process

  1. Safety Assessment: When we arrive, our first priority is safety. We assess the situation, ensure the area is safe, and if necessary, shut off the gas supply to prevent any risk while we work.
  2. Leak Detection Equipment: We use professional electronic gas detection equipment to locate the exact source of the leak. This equipment can detect gas concentrations far below what the human nose can sense, finding even hidden or underground leaks.
  3. Isolate the Leak: Once located, we isolate the affected section of the gas line so the rest of your system can continue operating safely while we complete the repair.
  4. Repair or Replace: We repair or replace the damaged section using code-approved materials and methods. For corroded or aging lines, we may recommend replacing larger sections to prevent future issues.
  5. Pressure Test: After the repair, we perform a pressure test on the entire gas line system. This involves pressurizing the line and monitoring for any pressure drop over a set period — confirming there are zero leaks anywhere in the system.
  6. Safety Verification: We verify every gas appliance is reconnected properly, pilot lights are lit, and the entire system is functioning safely. We don't leave until we're 100% confident your home is safe.

Gas Line FAQs

Common questions about gas line services.

If you smell gas, leave the area immediately. Do not turn on or off any lights, appliances, or electronics — a spark could ignite the gas. Once you are safely outside and away from the building, call 911 and then call us at (530) 343-7770. Do not re-enter the building until a professional has confirmed it is safe.

Gas leak detection typically costs $150-$300 depending on the complexity and accessibility of your gas lines. If we detect a leak and you proceed with the repair, the detection cost is often rolled into the overall repair quote. We always provide upfront pricing before beginning any work.

New gas line installation typically ranges from $500-$2,000+ depending on the distance, material, and complexity. A short run for a gas dryer or stove is on the lower end, while a longer line to an outdoor kitchen or pool heater costs more. We provide a detailed, upfront quote after assessing your specific needs.

Gas appliances typically cost less to operate (natural gas is cheaper than electricity per BTU), heat faster, and provide more precise temperature control — especially for cooking and water heating. Electric appliances are simpler to install and don't require gas line infrastructure. If you already have gas lines, gas appliances are usually the more economical choice. We can help you evaluate the best option for your home.
