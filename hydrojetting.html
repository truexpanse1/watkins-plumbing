Hydro Jetting vs Snaking: The Ultimate Solution for Stubborn Clogs

If you're searching for hydro jetting near me, Watkins Plumbing offers the most effective high-pressure drain cleaning method available today. Using a specialized nozzle that blasts water at pressures up to 4,000 PSI, hydro jetting doesn't just punch through clogs — it scours the entire interior of the pipe, removing grease, scale, mineral deposits, tree roots, and years of accumulated buildup.

Understanding hydro jetting vs snaking is important: unlike traditional snaking, which only creates a hole through the blockage, hydro jetting restores your pipes to near-original condition. This means the clog won't come back in a few weeks or months. It's the difference between poking a hole through the problem and actually eliminating it — and the hydro jetting cost is often worth it for the longer-lasting results.

Commercial hydro jetting is particularly effective for restaurant kitchens dealing with grease buildup, older homes with decades of scale accumulation, and properties where hydro jetting roots from the sewer line is needed. Our technicians are trained to select the right pressure and nozzle configuration for your specific pipe material and condition, ensuring thorough cleaning without any risk of damage.

At Watkins Plumbing, we always start with a camera inspection before hydro jetting. This allows us to assess the condition of your pipes, identify the type of blockage, and confirm the pipe is structurally sound enough for high-pressure drain cleaning. After jetting, we run the camera again to verify the line is completely clear.

Signs You Need High-Pressure Drain Cleaning

  • Recurring clogs — If the same drain keeps clogging despite repeated snaking, the underlying buildup hasn't been removed
  • Slow drains despite cleaning — Drains that stay sluggish even after professional cleaning indicate heavy buildup coating the pipe walls
  • Commercial kitchen drains — Restaurant and commercial kitchen drains accumulate grease rapidly and benefit from regular hydro jetting
  • Tree roots near pipes — Hydro jetting can cut through root intrusions and clear them from the line completely
  • Buildup in old pipes — Older cast iron, clay, or galvanized pipes often have decades of mineral scale and corrosion buildup that only high-pressure water can remove

Our Residential & Commercial Hydro Jetting Process

  1. Camera Inspection First: Before we jet, we run a fiber optic camera through your line to assess the pipe condition, identify the type of blockage, and confirm the pipe can safely handle high-pressure cleaning.
  2. Select Pressure: Based on the pipe material, diameter, and blockage type, we select the appropriate water pressure and nozzle. PVC, clay, cast iron, and ABS all have different tolerances — we know exactly what each can handle.
  3. Blast Through Blockage: The hydro jetting nozzle is inserted into the pipe and advanced through the line. High-pressure water jets (up to 4,000 PSI) scour the entire pipe circumference, blasting through grease, roots, scale, and debris.
  4. Re-Inspect: After jetting, we run the camera through again so you can see the results. You'll see clean pipe walls and full flow restored — proof the job is done right.
  5. Verify Flow: We run water through the entire system to confirm flow is fully restored and make recommendations for a maintenance schedule to keep your lines clean long-term.

Hydro Jetting FAQs

Common questions about our hydro jetting service.

Snaking uses a rotating cable to punch a hole through a clog, restoring some flow but leaving buildup on the pipe walls. Hydro jetting uses high-pressure water (up to 4,000 PSI) to scour the entire interior of the pipe, removing all grease, scale, roots, and debris. Hydro jetting provides a much more thorough, longer-lasting clean.
