Professional Sewer Camera Inspection in Chico & Butte County

When you're dealing with recurring plumbing problems, guesswork isn't good enough. Our high-resolution drain camera inspection system is mounted on a flexible rod that travels through your pipes, transmitting live video to a monitor so both you and our technician can see exactly what's going on. Whether it's tree root intrusion, a collapsed pipe, buildup, or a misaligned joint, our video pipe inspection technology will find it and show it to you on screen.

A sewer scope inspection saves you money by pinpointing the exact location and nature of the problem before any digging begins. Many homeowners are surprised at how affordable the plumbing camera inspection cost is compared to the thousands saved by avoiding unnecessary excavation.

We recommend camera inspections for homebuyers completing a real estate sewer inspection, recurring drain issues, pre-renovation checks, and anytime you need answers about the condition of your sewer or drain lines. A camera inspection for home buying is the smartest first step — it gives you negotiating power and peace of mind before closing.

Signs You Need a Drain Camera Inspection

  • Recurring clogs that keep coming back despite cleaning
  • You're buying a new home and want to verify pipe condition
  • Unexplained wet spots or soggy areas in your yard
  • Concern about tree roots growing near your sewer line
  • Old pipes (pre-1970) that may be deteriorating
  • Previous sewer problems or repairs that need follow-up

Our Video Pipe Inspection Process

  1. Insert the Camera: We feed our high-definition fiber optic camera into the cleanout or drain opening, carefully navigating your pipe system.
  2. Live Video Feed: You watch the real-time video with our technician as we inspect every inch of your sewer line on a high-resolution monitor.
  3. Identify the Issue: We locate cracks, root intrusion, blockages, bellies, offsets, or any other problems — and mark their exact location.
  4. Provide Footage & Report: You receive a copy of the video footage along with a detailed written report of our findings.
  5. Recommend a Solution: Based on what the camera reveals, we give you an honest recommendation with upfront pricing for any needed repairs.
